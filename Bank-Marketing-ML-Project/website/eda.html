📌 What is EDA?

Why we explore before we model

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is the process of analyzing datasets to summarize their main characteristics — using visual methods and statistics before applying any ML model.

In this project, EDA helped us understand:

  • How the target variable is distributed
  • Which customer demographics are most common
  • How financial features relate to subscription
  • How campaign behavior affects outcome
  • Which features are most correlated

👤 Step 5: Demographics Analysis

Who are the bank's customers?

💡

Key Insights

  • Age: Average age is 40.9 years. Most customers are between 30–40 years old. Older customers (60+) show higher subscription rates.
  • Job: Most common jobs are blue-collar (22%), management (25%), and technicians (15%).
  • Marital Status: Married customers dominate (~60%), but single customers have slightly higher subscription rates.
  • Education: Customers with tertiary education are more likely to subscribe to a term deposit.

💰 Step 6: Financial Features Analysis

How do balance and loans affect subscription?

💡

Key Insights

  • Balance: Average balance is €1,362.3. Customers with higher account balances tend to subscribe more — they have more financial security to invest.
  • Housing Loan: 55.6% have a housing loan. Customers without a housing loan are more likely to subscribe — less financial burden.
  • Personal Loan: 16.0% have a personal loan. Customers without a personal loan show higher subscription rates.
  • Credit Default: Very few customers have credit defaults, but those without are more likely to subscribe.
Key Takeaway: Financial freedom is a key predictor — customers with no existing debt are better prospects.

📞 Step 7: Campaign Features Analysis

How does the campaign itself affect the outcome?

💡

Key Insights

  • Duration: Average call duration is 258.2 seconds (~4.3 minutes). Longer call duration strongly correlates with subscription — the longer the call, the higher the chance of conversion.
  • Month: Calls in March, September, October, December have higher success rates. These months align with financial planning periods.
  • Campaign Count: Average 2.8 contacts per campaign. Customers contacted fewer times are more likely to subscribe — too many calls leads to rejection.
  • Previous Outcome: If the previous campaign was a success, the customer is much more likely to subscribe again.

🔥 Step 8: Correlation Heatmap

How do features relate to each other?

💡

Key Insights

  • Duration ↔ Target (y): Strongest positive correlation with target — most important feature for prediction.
  • pdays ↔ previous: Highly correlated — both relate to prior campaign contact history. Expected behavior.
  • Age ↔ balance: Mild positive correlation — older customers tend to have more savings.
  • Low Multicollinearity: Most features show low correlation — good for model stability and interpretability.
No severe multicollinearity detected — all features can be safely used in modeling.
